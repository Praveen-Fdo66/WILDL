When is the best time to visit Yala National Park

As we describe in our '39 things to know before visiting Sri Lanka' essay, the island has not one, but two monsoon seasons each year, which affect separate sides of the island at different times of the year. The dry season, which lasts from February to July, is truly the only time to visit Yala National Park, as a shortage of water means lots of species to be observed around reservoirs. We visited in November, just as the park reopened, and while it was lush with green from the northeastern monsoon, we recommend a Yala National Park safari between February and July, when wildlife viewing opportunities are better due to a lack of water



Want to see a leopard? Between April and July, water levels are low, making leopards more active and visible. However, we did see one during our November vacation. Bird enthusiasts may favor the months of November through January, when there are many migratory birds to witness.



Finally, the park can get fairly crowded (and we mean crowded!) between December and April, so if you want a more serene Yala National Park safari experience, go during the off-season. (thecommonwanderer.com)

Regardless of the season, schedule your safari for early in the morning or late in the afternoon when the animals are most active.




NOTE | The park is closed for a month between September and October for annual maintenance/drought prevention.
